PF Auto Style — A workshop that finally looked like the work.

How we rebuilt a paint-protection-film specialist's brand and digital storefront over twelve weeks, and what we learned about how skilled trades can speak online.

  • Client PF Auto Style
  • Location West Palm Beach, FL
  • Engagement 12 weeks · full build
  • Year 2025

— Chapter 01

The Brief

PF Auto Style does some of the cleanest paint-protection-film installations on the Treasure Coast. The work is meticulous — measured to the millimeter, finished at midnight. The website that represented it was a 2017 Wix template.

The owner came to us with a clear ask: make the site look like what people see when they actually pick up their car. Premium clientele weren't booking online. Most leads came from referrals or social. The site was a liability, not an asset.

  • Constraint Three-week wireframe phase, eight weeks to ship.
  • Constraint Existing customers expected the brand to feel like the shop.
  • Constraint Local SEO had to hold — the legacy site ranked.
— Chapter 02

Approach

Three movements: strategy, brand language, then a single-night launch with redirects locked. We don't believe in soft launches.

  1. 01

    Strategy — what the site needs to do, in plain English.

    Three-hour working session in the shop. We mapped the customer journey from "I just bought a new GR Corolla" to "scheduled for Tuesday at 8am." The site exists to make that journey shorter, calmer, and obvious.

  2. 02

    Brand language — the shop is the brand.

    Industrial-editorial direction. Precision telemetry as design pattern. Process documentation photography (real, shot in the bay) replaced stock photos. Type tightened to a single serif + a single mono.

  3. 03

    Build & launch — hand-coded, no CMS.

    Static site on a CDN. Page-load under 1.2s on cellular. Booking flow integrates with Calendly. Redirects mapped one-to-one from the Wix legacy to preserve every backlink.

[ Before · After ] 90 days apart
— Chapter 03

What we learned

Skilled trades have a copywriting problem. The work is technical, precise, and visible to the trained eye. The site has to translate that to people who only know what their car looks like.

— Clarisa, founder

The hardest part of this engagement wasn't motion or layout. It was finding language that matched the precision of the work without sounding clinical. We rewrote the homepage hero seven times before we shipped.

What landed: short declarative sentences, real numbers (mil thickness, hours per panel, year on the warranty), and one unambiguous CTA per page. Anyone in the trade reading this will know exactly what we mean.
